    Introduction

    In the following report, I present the key findings I've obtained from analyzing a Ford GoBike dataset. The goal of the project was to deliver Ford an in-depth analysis of their bike service data, providing them with meaningful insights to guide future business decisions.

    The project addressed the following research question:

    What are common characteristics of the consumer base and which variables are associated with demand at the top 10 starting stations?

    The project focused on addressing Ford's organizational needs by extracting actionable insights from the dataset, which will help them implement targeted marketing strategies and meet demand effectively.

    Dataset Overview and Summary

    The dataset used in this analysis contains information about individual rides from the Ford GoBike service in the San Francisco Bay Area. The cleaned dataset consists of 174,880 entries and 12 columns, covering rides that occurred from February 01, 2019 to March 01, 2019.

    • Sunday and Saturday consistently see the least amount of rides compared to the weekdays for all involved stations.
    • Sanfrancisco Caltrain Station 2 experiences a peak in rides on Tuesdays and Thursdays.

    Action: Ford BikeShare can leverage this data by increasing bike availability at popular stations during weekdays to accomodate the rise in demand. They should adjust the distribution of bikes based on weekday demand patterns to ensure availability and manage demand. Additionally, they should schedule bike maintenance and repairs during the weekends to minimize disruption during the busy weekdays.

    Ford should consider offering premium services for an additional fee during weekdays at popular stations. Premium services may include bikes with special features (built-in phone chargers, GPS) or bike reservations.

    Overall, bike reservations should be offered during weekdays and peak hours at the top 10 start stations to boost revenue.
